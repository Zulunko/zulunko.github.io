The goal state for the domain is simple: the three players need to be at the ComingHome location, which they can only get to by disabling or destroying the monolith.

We separated actions into rough categories to make it easier to browse. These categories do not exist in the planning process.

First, we have 9 crafting actions. Because crafting has specific output based on the materials used, every crafting action has to be defined separately; there is no "general" crafting action. Nonetheless, all of these actions are similar: they take various materials and "produce" something else.

Next, we have four movement-related actions. Each action is designed to move the entire team as a unit; this way, the solution plan is more readable since it contains a quarter as many move actions. First, we have a generic "proceed" action which is used to move from place to place once all players are ready to move. The other three actions are the act-specific actions which cause the players to be ready to move. They are separate actions because they have their own distinct preconditions; for example, in Act 1 the boat simply needs to not be defended for the group to proceed to Act 2, but in Act 3 the Monolith must be deactivated or destroyed before the group can proceed to ComingHome.

We classify the next group of actions as "inhabitant-related" as they relate to at least the rough concept of NPCs who inhabit the domain (though the NPCs aren't actually characters in the planning process). This category is rather arbitrary and simply gives us a way to not have our "all remaining actions" table be massive.
